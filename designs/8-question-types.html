Spot the Flaw Strength of Materials

"The cylindrical vessel has inner radius r = 500 mm and wall thickness t = 10 mm under internal pressure p = 2 MPa. I calculated the hoop stress as sigma = pr/t = 100 MPa. Since the axial stress is the same as hoop stress in a cylinder, the von Mises equivalent stress is also 100 MPa."
A The hoop stress formula pr/t is only valid for thick-walled vessels
B Axial stress is pr/(2t), not pr/t -- it is half the hoop stress
C The vessel should be analyzed using Lame's equations instead
D Von Mises stress cannot be applied to pressure vessels
What Fails First Materials Science

A bolted flange joint connects two steel pipes carrying pressurized steam at 450 C. After 3 years of continuous service, which component is most likely to fail first?

The joint uses: A193 B7 alloy steel bolts, spiral wound gasket with graphite filler, A105 carbon steel flanges. Bolt preload was set correctly at installation.
A Bolts -- creep relaxation reduces clamping force over time at this temperature
B Gasket -- graphite degrades and loses sealing ability above 400 C
C Flanges -- carbon steel loses strength significantly at 450 C
D Pipe wall -- erosion-corrosion thins the wall near the joint
