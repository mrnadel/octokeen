Core issue: Free users have access to 100% of the learning content and all practice modes. The only friction is a soft heart system that can be bypassed by waiting (4h per heart) or spending earned gems (15 gems per heart, ~3 gems per daily quest). There is no compelling reason a patient user would ever need to pay.

Why Duolingo Gets 9% and We Won't (Yet)

Duolingo Has

  • Aggressive interstitial ads (biggest converter)
  • Nerfed gem-based heart refills in most markets
  • Content progression walls on later skills
  • 750M+ total users (scale effects)
  • Family plan & student discounts
  • Max Tier ($14/mo) with AI features

Octokeen Lacks

  • No ads at all (no annoyance-driven conversion)
  • Cheap heart refills via gems (15 gems = ~5 quests)
  • All content free (no progression wall)
  • Niche audience (smaller pool)
  • Single plan (no tier upsell)
  • No AI features to differentiate Pro

Conversion Optimization Roadmap

Complete these in order. Each phase builds on the previous one. The conversion estimate assumes all items in the phase are done.

1
Tighten the Economy
Make free tier feel free, not unlimited. No new features needed, just tuning numbers.
5-7%
Expected rate
  • Increase heart refill gem cost: 15 → 35 gems per heart
    Currently a free user earns ~3 gems per daily quest, so 5 quests = 1 free heart. At 35 gems, a full refill costs 175 gems, roughly a week of quest grinding. Makes waiting vs. paying a real tradeoff.
    High
  • Reduce heart recharge time from 4 hours to 5 hours
    Extends full recharge from 20 hours to 25 hours. Free users who run out mid-session wait longer. Subtle but compounds with the gem cost increase.
    Med
  • Make streak repair Pro-only (remove 75-gem repair for free users)
    Losing a 30-day streak with no recovery option is the strongest emotional conversion trigger. Free users keep the freeze purchase (40 gems) but lose repair after the fact.
    High
  • Reduce daily quest gem rewards: 3 → 2 gems per quest
    Slows the free gem accumulation rate. Users earn ~10 gems/day (down from ~15) from daily quests. Makes the gem economy tighter without feeling punitive.
    Med
  • Add contextual upgrade prompts at emotional moments
    Show upgrade modal after: running out of hearts mid-lesson, losing a streak, completing a streak milestone (7/14/30 days), getting promoted in league. These are moments when users feel invested.
    Med
2
Content Gating
The single biggest conversion lever. Gate later content behind Pro.
7-10%
Expected rate
  • Lock Units 5-10 behind Pro subscription
    Free users get Units 1-4 (Statics, Dynamics, Strength of Materials, Thermodynamics). That's 78 lessons, enough to validate the product. Units 5-10 (Heat Transfer, Fluids, Materials, Machine Design, GD&T, Interview Prep) require Pro. This mirrors how Duolingo locks later skills.
    High
  • Show locked units on the course map with a Pro badge
    Users should see Units 5-10 on the map but grayed out with a lock icon and "Pro" tag. Visibility creates desire. Let them tap to see a preview of what the unit covers, then show the upgrade modal.
    High
  • Gate "Interview Prep" practice mode behind Pro
    This is your most valuable practice mode for the target audience (ME interview prep). Free users can practice by topic, but the interview simulation mode requires Pro. High perceived value.
    High
  • Allow one free "preview lesson" per locked unit
    Let free users try the first lesson of each locked unit. After completing it, show "Enjoyed this? Unlock the full unit with Pro." This is the taste-then-gate pattern.
    Med
3
Pro Experience & Trial
Make Pro feel premium. Let users taste it, then take it away.
10-14%
Expected rate
  • Add a 7-day free trial of Pro
    Let new users experience unlimited hearts, all content, and full analytics for 7 days. After the trial, they revert to free. Loss aversion is the strongest psychological conversion driver. Paddle supports trial periods natively.
    High
  • Build an "Interview Readiness Score" (Pro-only detailed breakdown)
    Show all users a single number: "Interview Readiness: 67%". Free users see the score. Pro users see the full breakdown: weak topics, recommended practice plan, topic-by-topic confidence chart. Creates a visible reason to upgrade.
    High
  • Add detailed explanations as a Pro perk
    Currently listed as a Pro feature in pricing but may not be fully differentiated. Free users get "Correct/Incorrect" with a brief note. Pro users get step-by-step worked solutions with diagrams. This is high value for interview prep.
    Med
  • Monthly-to-annual upgrade prompt after 2 months of Pro
    Users paying $7.99/mo for 2+ months should see a prompt: "You've been Pro for 2 months ($16 spent). Switch to annual and save 48%." Reduces churn and locks in revenue.
    Med
  • Add push notifications for re-engagement
    You have web-push in your dependencies. Use it: streak at risk (20h since last session), daily quests expiring, league about to reset. Bring users back before they lapse. Earlier comebacks = more sessions = more heart-wall hits = more conversions.
    Med
  • Trigger comeback flow at 24-48 hours (currently 3+ days)
    The comeback quests mechanic is good but triggers too late. By day 3, most lapsed users are gone. Trigger at 24h absence with a push notification and at 48h with the comeback quest flow.
    Low
4
Advanced Monetization
Optional growth levers once the core funnel is optimized.
12-16%
Potential ceiling
  • Add AI-powered mock interview mode (Pro exclusive)
    Use an LLM to simulate a real ME interview conversation. Ask follow-up questions, challenge answers, give feedback. This is the highest-value Pro feature you could build and would differentiate Octokeen from any static quiz app.
    High
  • Introduce a "Pro Max" tier at $14.99/mo
    Everything in Pro plus AI mock interviews, priority support, and exclusive cosmetics. Creates an anchor that makes $7.99 feel cheap. Even if few users pick Max, it boosts standard Pro conversion through the decoy effect.
    Med
  • Team/university plans
    Sell bulk licenses to ME departments and study groups. $5/user/mo for 10+ seats. Different acquisition channel but high LTV and low churn (institutional buyers).
    Med
  • Add a referral program (give a month, get a month)
    ME students know other ME students. Give existing Pro users a way to invite friends: both get 1 month of Pro free. Reduces CAC to zero for referred users and boosts word-of-mouth in a tight-knit community.
    Low

The math problem: A free user grinding all quests earns ~290 gems/week. A full heart refill costs 60 gems. That's nearly 5 full refills per week from quests alone, which means 25 extra hearts per week on top of natural recharge (roughly 30 hearts/week). Free users can sustain ~55 hearts/week, enough for 55+ lessons with zero wrong answers, or ~30 lessons at a 50% accuracy rate. Pro is a nice-to-have, not a need.
