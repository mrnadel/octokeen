SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Unique positioning — only gamified, mobile-first, interview-specific ME prep tool in existence
  • Production-ready product — 11 topics, 12 question types, 10 course units, Stripe integration, auth, adaptive engine
  • Domain authenticity — built by a 6-year ME who lived the interview process
  • Smart pricing — $9/mo sits in a sweet spot below Brilliant ($25) and far below Skill-Lync ($250+)
  • Low overhead — indie developer, no VC pressure, profitable at small user counts

Weaknesses

  • Solo developer bottleneck — content creation, dev, marketing, and support all on one person
  • No brand recognition yet — zero existing audience or distribution
  • Content volume — question bank needs continuous expansion before power users exhaust it
  • No native mobile app — responsive web works but lacks push notifications and App Store discovery
  • No AI integration — hand-curated is higher quality but slower to scale than AI-generated

Opportunities

  • LeetEngineer validates demand — just launched on HN, proving others see the gap too
  • B2B / university channel — career services offices and corporate training need this
  • FE/PE exam adjacency — adding an FE prep mode could 2x the addressable market
  • Company-specific packs — "Prep for Tesla" / "Prep for Boeing" is extremely high-value differentiation
  • AI-assisted content scaling — use AI to draft, hand-curate for quality, build a content moat
  • Localization — Hebrew, Hindi, German markets have near-zero competition

Threats

  • AI commoditization — ChatGPT can generate ME questions for free; must offer more than raw content
  • Well-funded copycat risk — if a VC-backed team copies this concept with a bigger team
  • Brilliant.org expansion — $92M funded, 10M users; if they pivot to interview prep, they're serious
  • Short prep cycles — interview prep is 2-4 weeks; retention/churn could be challenging
  • Content accuracy — wrong answers in engineering context can destroy credibility fast
